## Cylinder Head Components

### Component #1: Cylinder Head

**Description:** Aluminum alloy, houses valves, camshafts, and combustion chambers

**Location:** Top of engine block

**Visual Guide:**
- [EricTheCarGuy - Cylinder Head Removal](https://www.ericthecarguy.com/mini-cooper-n14-bent-valve-repair-part-1/)
- [Pelican Parts - Valve Cover Removal](https://www.pelicanparts.com/techarticles/MINI_R56/81-ENGINE-Valve_Cover_Replacement/)

**Specific Failures:**

#### Failure 1.1: Warped Cylinder Head
**Cause:** Overheating, blown head gasket
**Symptoms:**
- Coolant in oil (milky appearance)
- Oil in coolant
- White smoke from exhaust
- Overheating
- Compression loss

**Testing:**
- Straight edge and feeler gauge test
- Compression test (should be 12-14 bar per cylinder)
- Leak-down test

**Repair:** Resurface or replace head
**Cost:** R8,000-15,000 (resurface) / R15,000-30,000 (replace)
**Mileage:** Can occur anytime after overheating

#### Failure 1.2: Cracked Cylinder Head
**Cause:** Extreme overheating, thermal stress
**Symptoms:**
- External coolant leak
- Compression loss between cylinders
- Misfires
- White smoke

**Testing:**
- Pressure test
- Dye penetrant test
- Magnaflux inspection

**Repair:** Replace cylinder head
**Cost:** R25,000-45,000
**Mileage:** Usually after severe overheating event

#### Failure 1.3: Valve Seat Recession
**Cause:** Poor quality fuel, overheating, excessive carbon
**Symptoms:**
- Loss of compression gradually
- Hard starting
- Misfires
- Poor performance

**Testing:**
- Compression test shows gradual decrease
- Leak-down test shows valve leakage

**Repair:** Machine new valve seats
**Cost:** R8,000-15,000 (included in head rebuild)
**Mileage:** 160,000-240,000 km (high mileage issue)

### Component #2: Intake Valves (16 total - 4 per cylinder)

**Description:** Allow air/fuel mixture into combustion chamber

**Location:** Cylinder head intake ports

**Specific Failures:**

#### Failure 2.1: Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves (VERY COMMON)
**Cause:** Direct injection (no fuel washing valves), oil vapor from PCV
**Symptoms:**
- Rough idle
- Hesitation
- Loss of power
- Poor fuel economy
- Misfires
- **Can occur as early as 24,000 km!**

**Testing:**
- Borescope inspection through spark plug holes
- Black carbon deposits visible

**Repair:** Walnut blasting service
**Cost:** R3,000-6,000
**Frequency:** Every 48,000-64,000 km recommended
**Visual:** [YouTube - "Walnut Blasting N14 intake valves"]

#### Failure 2.2: Bent Intake Valves
**Cause:** Timing chain failure (valves hit pistons), dropped valve
**Symptoms:**
- Sudden loss of power
- Loud metallic knocking
- Won't start
- No compression in affected cylinder

**Testing:**
- Compression test (0 or very low)
- Borescope inspection
- Cylinder head removal for visual inspection

**Repair:** Replace valves, possible piston damage
**Cost:** R15,000-40,000 (depends on extent)
**Mileage:** Usually after timing chain failure

**Visual Guide:** [EricTheCarGuy - Bent Valve Repair](https://www.ericthecarguy.com/mini-cooper-n14-bent-valve-repair-part-1/)

#### Failure 2.3: Burned Intake Valves
**Cause:** Lean running, overheating, incorrect timing
**Symptoms:**
- Loss of compression in one cylinder
- Misfire on that cylinder
- Poor performance
- Check engine light

**Testing:**
- Compression test
- Leak-down test (air escapes through intake)

**Repair:** Replace valve(s)
**Cost:** R8,000-20,000 (head work required)

### Component #3: Exhaust Valves (16 total - 4 per cylinder)

**Description:** Allow exhaust gases out of combustion chamber

**Location:** Cylinder head exhaust ports

**Specific Failures:**

#### Failure 3.1: Burned Exhaust Valves
**Cause:** Lean running, carbon deposits, overheating
**Symptoms:**
- Misfire on affected cylinder
- Loss of power
- Popping noise from exhaust
- Check engine light

**Testing:**
- Compression test (low on affected cylinder)
- Leak-down test (air escapes through exhaust)

**Repair:** Replace valve(s), lap seats
**Cost:** R8,000-20,000
**Mileage:** 120,000-200,000 km

#### Failure 3.2: Valve Stem Seal Failure
**Cause:** Heat, age, oil quality
**Symptoms:**
- Blue smoke on startup
- Blue smoke when decelerating
- Oil consumption (not as much as turbo seal)
- Fouled spark plugs

**Testing:**
- Visible blue smoke
- Oil in intake tract
- Compression test normal

**Repair:** Replace valve stem seals
**Cost:** R6,000-12,000
**Mileage:** 120,000-180,000 km

### Component #4: Valve Cover (Rocker Cover)

**Description:** Seals top of cylinder head

**Location:** Top of engine, easily visible

**Visual Guide:**
- [Pelican Parts - Valve Cover Replacement](https://www.pelicanparts.com/techarticles/MINI_R56/81-ENGINE-Valve_Cover_Replacement/81-ENGINE-Valve_Cover_Replacement.htm)
- [1A Auto - YouTube Video](https://www.1aauto.com/how-to-replace-valve-cover-2007-2013-mini-cooper/video/57188)

**Specific Failures:**

#### Failure 4.1: Valve Cover Gasket Leak (VERY COMMON)
**Cause:** Heat cycles, age, PCV pressure
**Symptoms:**
- Oil leak from top of engine
- Oil smell when engine hot
- Oil visible around spark plug tubes
- Low oil level

**Testing:**
- Visual inspection (oil seepage at edges)
- Clean engine, run, re-inspect

**Repair:** Replace gasket and grommets
**Cost:** R1,200-3,000
**Mileage:** 80,000-120,000 km
**Parts:** Gasket kit ~R400-800

#### Failure 4.2: Valve Cover Warped/Cracked
**Cause:** Over-tightening, heat stress
**Symptoms:**
- Oil leak that persists after gasket replacement
- Visible warping or cracks in cover

**Testing:**
- Straight edge measurement
- Visual inspection for cracks

**Repair:** Replace valve cover
**Cost:** R2,500-5,000 (OEM plastic cover)
**Aftermarket:** CNC aluminum cover available (R8,000-12,000)

#### Failure 4.3: PCV Diaphragm Failure (Inside Valve Cover)
**Cause:** Age, oil degradation, heat
**Symptoms:**
- Excessive oil consumption
- Oil in intake tract
- Rough idle
- Smoke from exhaust

**Testing:**
- Remove PCV system, inspect diaphragm
- Smoke test for leaks

**Repair:** Replace PCV valve/diaphragm
**Cost:** R800-2,000
**Mileage:** 80,000-120,000 km

## Valvetrain & Timing

### Component #5: Timing Chain

**Description:** Drives camshafts from crankshaft

**Location:** Front of engine, behind timing cover

**Visual Guide:**
- [FCP Euro - Timing Chain Replacement](https://www.fcpeuro.com/blog/how-to-replace-a-mini-r56-timing-chain-assembly)
- [YouTube - "N14 timing chain replacement"]

**Specific Failures:**

#### Failure 5.1: Timing Chain Stretch (CRITICAL - VERY COMMON)
**Cause:** Oil starvation, age, high mileage, poor oil changes
**Symptoms:**
- **"Death Rattle"** - loud rattling on cold start
- Rattling for 2-5 seconds then goes away
- Eventually continuous rattling
- Check engine light (timing codes)
- Loss of power

**Testing:**
- Listen for rattle on cold start
- Scan for P0016, P0008, P0009 codes
- Special tool measures chain stretch

**Repair:** Replace timing chain kit (ENTIRE KIT, not just chain)
**Cost:** R12,000-25,000
**Mileage:** 80,000-120,000 km typical failure
**CRITICAL:** 2007-2009 N14 engines MOST affected

**Parts Included in Kit:**
- Timing chain
- Tensioner (updated design)
- Upper guide rail
- Lower guide rail
- Gaskets

**Visual:**
- Very detailed photos at Pelican Parts and FCP Euro

#### Failure 5.2: Timing Chain Tensioner Failure (CRITICAL)
**Cause:** Design flaw, cannot maintain oil pressure
**Symptoms:**
- Identical to chain stretch (death rattle)
- Can fail suddenly

**Repair:** Must replace with updated tensioner
**Cost:** Included in timing chain kit
**Prevention:** Replace preventatively at 80,000 km

---

### Component #6: Timing Chain Guides

**Description:** Plastic rails that guide timing chain

**Location:** Inside timing cover, front of engine

**Specific Failures:**

#### Failure 6.1: Upper Guide Rail Cracking/Breaking
**Cause:** Chain slap from loose tensioner
**Symptoms:**
- Rattling noise
- Plastic pieces in oil pan
- Timing chain damage

**Repair:** Replace as part of timing chain kit
**Cost:** Included in R12,000-25,000 kit

#### Failure 6.2: Lower Guide Rail Wear
**Cause:** Normal wear, accelerated by loose chain
**Symptoms:**
- Often asymptomatic until failure
- Can cause sudden chain jump

**Repair:** Always replace with timing chain
**Cost:** Included in kit

### Component #7: Camshafts (2: Intake and Exhaust)

**Description:** Open and close valves

**Location:** Top of cylinder head, under valve cover

**Specific Failures:**

#### Failure 7.1: Camshaft Lobe Wear
**Cause:** Oil starvation, poor oil quality
**Symptoms:**
- Ticking noise
- Misfires
- Loss of power
- Check engine light

**Testing:**
- Valve cover removal, visual inspection
- Measure lobe height

**Repair:** Replace camshaft(s)
**Cost:** R8,000-18,000 per camshaft
**Mileage:** 180,000-240,000 km (rare on well-maintained engines)

#### Failure 7.2: Camshaft Position Sensor Gear Failure
**Cause:** Wear, manufacturing defect
**Symptoms:**
- Check engine light
- Rough running
- Won't start
- P0340/P0345 codes

**Repair:** Replace gear on camshaft
**Cost:** R2,000-5,000 (if just gear) / R10,000-20,000 (if cam damaged)

### Component #8: Valvetronic Motor (Variable Valve Lift)

**Description:** Electric motor that controls intake valve lift

**Location:** Front of cylinder head, driver's side

**Visual Location:** Visible with engine cover removed, cylindrical motor

**Specific Failures:**

#### Failure 8.1: Valvetronic Motor Failure
**Cause:** Internal motor wear, sensor failure
**Symptoms:**
- Limp mode
- Reduced power
- Rough idle
- P1018, P1014 codes
- May hear motor whining

**Testing:**
- Scan tool shows valvetronic position errors
- Listen for motor operation

**Repair:** Replace valvetronic motor
**Cost:** R6,000-12,000
**Mileage:** 120,000-180,000 km

#### Failure 8.2: Valvetronic Eccentric Shaft Seized
**Cause:** Lack of lubrication, carbon buildup
**Symptoms:**
- Same as motor failure
- Motor may burn out from excessive load

**Repair:** Clean and lubricate or replace
**Cost:** R8,000-15,000 (labor intensive)
