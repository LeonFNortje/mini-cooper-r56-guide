## Root Cause

**Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**

The N14 (2007-2010) uses a hydraulic timing chain tensioner that is **known to fail prematurely**.

**Failure Mechanism:**
1. Tensioner loses hydraulic pressure
2. Chain becomes loose
3. Chain slaps against timing cover ("death rattle")
4. Chain stretches
5. Chain guides break
6. Chain jumps timing or breaks
7. Valves contact pistons = **engine destroyed**

**Contributing Factors:**
- Low oil level (accelerates wear)
- Irregular oil changes
- High oil consumption (common on N14)
- Design flaw in early tensioners

## Diagnostic Steps

### Step 1: Listen for Rattle
**Test:** Cold start the engine

**Normal:** Quiet start, no unusual noises
**Abnormal:** Rattling for 2-3 seconds (early stage) or continuous (advanced)

**Location:** Front of engine, timing cover area (driver's side)

---

### Step 2: Check Oil Level
**Critical:** Low oil accelerates timing chain wear

**Procedure:**
1. Park on level ground
2. Engine warm, turn off, wait 5 minutes
3. Pull dipstick, wipe, reinsert, check

**Result:**
- Oil at MIN or below â†’ **Add oil immediately**
- Oil consumption high â†’ Monitor every 800 km

**Related:** [Oil System](oil-system.md)

---

### Step 3: Visual Inspection
**Access:** Remove engine cover

**Check for:**
- Oil leaks at timing cover
- Oil leaks at valve cover
- Visible timing cover damage

---

### Step 4: Scan for Codes
**Use:** OBD-II scanner

**Related Codes:**
- P0016: Camshaft/Crankshaft correlation
- P0008: Bank 1 timing over-advanced
- P0009: Bank 1 timing over-retarded
- P0340: Camshaft position sensor
- P0300-P0304: Misfires (from timing issues)

**Note:** Timing chain issues may or may not throw codes early on

---

### Step 5: Professional Inspection
**Recommended if rattling present**

**Requires:**
- Removal of valve cover
- Visual inspection of chain, guides, tensioner
- Check chain stretch with special tool

**Cost:** $200-400 for inspection

## Repair Options

### Option 1: Timing Chain Tensioner Only
**When:** Very early stage, confirmed tensioner only
**Cost:** $800-1,200
**Parts:** Tensioner only
**Risk:** Chain may already be stretched

**Not Recommended** - Band-aid fix

---

### Option 2: Full Timing Chain Kit
**When:** Any rattling present
**Cost:** $1,500-2,500
**Parts:**
- Timing chain
- Tensioner
- Upper guide rail
- Lower guide rail
- Gaskets

**Recommended** - Complete fix

**Additional:** Often done with:
- Vacuum pump replacement ($200-400 more)
- Water pump replacement ($200-400 more)
- Thermostat housing ($100-200 more)

---

### Option 3: Preventative Replacement
**When:** 2008-2009 model, no symptoms yet
**Cost:** Same as Option 2
**Benefit:** Peace of mind, prevents catastrophic failure

**Highly Recommended for 2008-2009 N14 engines**

## Labor Time

**Book Time:** 8-12 hours
**Actual Time:** 10-14 hours (varies by shop experience)

**Why so long?**
- Front of engine disassembly
- Requires special tools
- Timing marks must be precise
- Multiple gaskets to replace

## Prevention

### If You Haven't Replaced Yet:
1. **Check oil level WEEKLY**
2. Change oil every 8,000 km (not 10-15k)
3. Use quality 0W-40 synthetic
4. Listen for rattle at every start
5. Budget for replacement

### After Replacement:
1. Continue 5,000 mile oil changes
2. Monitor oil consumption
3. Use quality oil only
